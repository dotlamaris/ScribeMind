# Research Plan: Matcha‑Chocolate‑Coated Espresso Beans

## Goal
Create a reproducible home method for coating store‑bought espresso beans with matcha‑flavored chocolate while preserving bean shape and achieving a consistent coating ratio.

## Scope
- Ingredient selection (espresso beans, matcha chocolate, optional stabilizers)
- Coating technique (tempering, enrobing, dipping)
- Ratio & timing (chocolate‑to‑bean weight, coating thickness)
- Quality control (shape retention, texture, flavor balance)
- Storage & shelf life

## Steps & Timeline
| Phase | Tasks | Tools/Resources | ETA |
|------|-------|----------------|-----|
| 1️⃣ | Source high‑grade espresso beans (single‑origin, 12‑15 g) | Local roaster, online beans | Day 1 |
| 2️⃣ | Procure matcha chocolate (70 % cacao, 1‑2 % matcha powder) | Specialty chocolate maker, online suppliers | Day 1 |
| 3️⃣ | Test chocolate tempering methods (seed, tabling) | Thermometer, marble slab | Day 2 |
| 4️⃣ | Develop coating protocol: melt chocolate, stir in matcha, maintain 31‑32 °C | Double boiler, heat gun | Day 2‑3 |
| 5️⃣ | Enrobing technique: low‑viscosity dip, shake‑off excess, set on parchment | Silicone mat, cooling rack | Day 3‑4 |
| 6️⃣ | Measure ratio: target 0.25 g chocolate per bean (±0.02 g) | Precision scale | Day 4 |
| 7️⃣ | Evaluate bean integrity (shape, crunch) & flavor balance (taste panel) | Sensory checklist | Day 5 |
| 8️⃣ | Iterate adjustments (chocolate viscosity, matcha concentration) | Record observations | Day 5‑7 |
| 9️⃣ | Document final recipe & storage conditions (airtight container, 4 °C) | Write SOPs | Day 8 |

## Deliverables
- `matcha_espresso_procedure.docx` (full SOP)
- `coating_ratio_calibration.csv` (experiment log)
- Summary report in `matcha_espresso_research_findings.md`

The plan will be refined based on trial results and documented for reproducibility.
